# Discovery Plan Input — International Expansion

We're thinking about expanding into Latin America. Can you help me figure out what we'd need to learn first?

---

## Background

We're a B2B SaaS tool for restaurant inventory management. US-only today, about 2,200 restaurant customers, $8M ARR. The CEO mentioned at the last all-hands that LatAm is our next growth market because "restaurants everywhere have the same inventory problems" and a few inbound leads from Mexico and Brazil asked about localization.

Our investor has been pushing international expansion as the next growth lever. One of our board members has restaurant industry connections in Brazil.

---

## What I Know (Not Much)

- We've had 12 inbound inquiries from LatAm in the past 6 months (7 Mexico, 3 Brazil, 2 Colombia)
- We have zero localization — English-only, USD pricing, US tax/compliance integrations
- Our product assumes US supply chain patterns (specific distributor integrations, USDA food safety categories)
- Nobody on the team speaks Spanish or Portuguese
- We have no presence, partnerships, or brand recognition in any LatAm market

---

## The Ask

I don't even know what I don't know here. The CEO is enthusiastic but I'm not sure "restaurants everywhere have the same inventory problems" is actually true. Before we commit to a localization roadmap, what should we learn? I have maybe 6-8 weeks before the next board meeting where this will come up.
