# Discovery Plan Input — Enterprise Onboarding Drop-Off

Hey, I need to plan some research before we commit to a big onboarding redesign.

---

## What We Think Is Happening

We're seeing enterprise users (companies with 10+ seats) drop off during onboarding at a much higher rate than SMBs. Our aggregate funnel shows:

- Sign-up to first meaningful action: 62% for SMB users, but only 34% for enterprise
- 14-day retention: 71% SMB, 41% enterprise
- Enterprise accounts make up 15% of sign-ups but 38% of our revenue potential (based on contract values)

The product team thinks it's because our onboarding flow was designed for individual users and doesn't work well for teams — no bulk invite, no admin setup wizard, no role-based permissions in the initial flow. The VP of Product has been pushing for an "enterprise onboarding redesign" for two quarters now.

---

## What We Don't Know

Honestly, we don't really know *why* enterprise users are dropping off. The funnel data tells us *where* they drop, not *why*. Some possibilities:

- The onboarding flow genuinely doesn't work for teams (the product team's hypothesis)
- Enterprise buyers are evaluating us alongside 2-3 competitors and churning to a competitor, not because of onboarding
- The "enterprise" segment is too broad — a 10-person startup and a 500-person company probably have very different needs
- IT/procurement friction is the real blocker (SSO requirements, security reviews, vendor approval processes) and onboarding UX is irrelevant
- The champion who signed up can't get their team to adopt, and it's a change management problem, not a product problem

---

## Some Context

- We have Amplitude set up and can pull behavioral data, but nobody's done a deep dive on the enterprise onboarding funnel specifically
- We've never done formal user research with enterprise customers — just a few ad hoc conversations the CSM team has had
- Our CSM team handles accounts with 50+ seats and has qualitative signal, but nothing systematic
- We have a Slack community with ~400 members, skewed toward power users
- The VP wants a decision by end of Q2 (8 weeks from now) on whether to greenlight the redesign

---

## What I Need

Before I write a PRD for the redesign (or decide not to), I need a research plan. What should we learn, in what order, and how? I want to make sure we're solving the right problem before we commit engineering resources.
