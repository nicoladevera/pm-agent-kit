# Presentation Deck Input — VP of Product Review: Recommendation Algorithm Update

I need to present the results of our recommendation algorithm update to our VP of Product next week. Can you help me structure the narrative? She's data-first and wants the recommendation upfront. She won't sit through a build-up — she wants to know what to do, then see the evidence.

---

## What We Did

Three weeks ago we rolled out a significant change to our content recommendation algorithm — 100% of users, no holdback group. We shifted from a popularity-based model ("show everyone what's trending globally") to a personalized taste graph ("show each user content similar to what they've watched and rated highly").

---

## The Results

Here's what the data shows:

**Engagement wins:**
- Recommendation click-through rate improved from 8.2% to 11.4% — that's +3.2 percentage points, a 39% relative lift
- Average watch time per session increased from 42 minutes to 47 minutes — +12%

**The problem:**
- Subscription conversion rate from free trial dropped from 6.1% to 5.4% — that's -0.7 percentage points
- The hypothesis we're working with: the personalization is so good at satisfying users with free content that they don't feel the urgency to subscribe. We call this internally the "satisfaction trap."

**Neutral:**
- Monthly active users are essentially flat: +0.3%, not statistically significant

---

## What My VP Will Want to Know

My VP, Priya, is very data-driven. She will:
- Expect the recommendation upfront, not after a 10-slide setup
- Ask "is this a causal relationship?" about the conversion drop — she'll want to know if we have evidence it's the algorithm vs. something else
- Want to know what we're going to do about the conversion drop, not just that it exists
- Be skeptical of any framing that sounds like spin. If I lead with the wins and bury the conversion drop, she'll notice and it will undermine my credibility

Priya needs to decide: **keep the algorithm update, roll it back, or run a follow-up experiment.** That's the decision this presentation is driving toward.

---

## What I'd Like to Do

I want to lead with the engagement wins — the CTR and watch time improvements are real and significant. But I recognize I probably can't bury the conversion drop and expect Priya not to notice.

Can you help me build a narrative that's honest about the tension between the engagement gains and the conversion risk? The engagement data is genuinely good. The conversion drop is real and I don't fully understand it yet. I need a narrative that leads with a clear recommendation, doesn't spin the conversion risk, and explains what we'd need to do to resolve the uncertainty.

---

## My Tentative Recommendation

Keep the update — don't roll back. The engagement gains are substantial and rolling back feels like a step backward. But we need to run an experiment to understand and address the conversion drop. I can't fully defend "keep it" without a plan for fixing the conversion problem.
