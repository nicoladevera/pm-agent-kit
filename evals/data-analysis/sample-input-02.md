# Data Question: What's going on with our DAU/WAU ratio?

Our DAU/WAU ratio dropped from 0.42 to 0.31 over the past month. What does this mean, and should I be worried?

I'm presenting our engagement metrics to the board next week and I want to make sure I understand what's actually happening before I walk in there. If the story is bad, I need to know. If there's a defensible explanation, I need to have it down cold.

**Context:** We're a consumer fitness/wellness subscription app. DAU/WAU is our primary engagement health metric — it tells us what fraction of weekly-active users are coming back on any given day. Higher is better: 0.42 was a good signal that a meaningful share of weekly users were making us part of their daily routine.

**The data — 8 weeks of DAU and WAU:**

| Week | WAU | DAU | DAU/WAU Ratio | Notes |
|------|-----|-----|---------------|-------|
| Week 1 | 179,000 | 75,200 | 0.42 | Baseline |
| Week 2 | 181,000 | 76,000 | 0.42 | Baseline |
| Week 3 | 180,500 | 75,800 | 0.42 | Baseline |
| Week 4 | 182,000 | 76,500 | 0.42 | Baseline |
| Week 5 | 210,000 | 82,000 | 0.39 | Apple App Store feature promotion ran this week |
| Week 6 | 198,000 | 63,500 | 0.32 | Push notification frequency reduced ~40% (went live start of week 6) |
| Week 7 | 201,000 | 62,000 | 0.31 | |
| Week 8 | 200,500 | 62,200 | 0.31 | Most recent full week |

**What happened during this period:**

Two things changed, roughly back-to-back:

1. **App Store feature promotion (week 5):** We got featured by Apple in the Health & Fitness category. This drove a big spike in downloads — WAU jumped from ~181K to 210K in a single week. The promotion ran for approximately one week.

2. **Push notification frequency reduction (start of week 6):** We reduced push notification frequency by approximately 40% — from an average of ~7 pushes/week per user down to ~4 pushes/week. This was a deliberate decision based on user feedback about notification fatigue. It went live at the start of week 6.

**What I'm specifically asking:** I know the ratio dropped. What I need to understand is: what's the actual story here — what drove the change? Is this a sign that engagement has gotten worse, or is there something else going on? I want to make sure I understand this before presenting it to the board next week.

I don't have cohort-level data broken out by acquisition source for this analysis. I have the WAU and DAU time series and the two context items above.
