# Decision Input: Native App vs. Mobile Web

**Source:** PM download + stakeholder alignment request

---

**Note from PM:**

I need to structure our native app vs. mobile web decision. Leadership is split and we need to make a call in 2 weeks. Here's where we are:

---

**Context:**

We're a B2B project management tool — think task tracking, project timelines, team assignments. Our users are primarily knowledge workers at mid-market companies. We launched 18 months ago and we're currently pre-Series B.

About 40% of our users access the product on mobile. But here's the thing — our mobile session lengths are 3x shorter than desktop sessions. Desktop averages 22 minutes. Mobile averages 7 minutes. I've been staring at this data for a month and my read is that people are checking status on mobile — "is that task done yet?" — not doing deep work. They're not creating projects or restructuring timelines on mobile. They're peeking.

Our competitors: two of our three main competitors (Asana and Monday.com equivalents) have native iOS and Android apps. One competitor is mobile-web-only and actually markets it as "no install friction, works on any device." So there's a split in the market.

Engineering estimate from our Eng Lead, Priya: building an iOS MVP would take 4-5 months and needs 2 engineers. Ongoing maintenance would be 1 engineer dedicated indefinitely. Android would be a separate build after iOS, probably another 4 months. We haven't scoped a PWA option at all.

The CFO, David, is skeptical: "We're pre-Series B. Native app is a lot of engineering budget for a feature segment of users who are just checking in." He's not opposed to mobile investment but he wants to understand the ROI.

The VP Product, Rachel, is an advocate: "We need a native app to be taken seriously in enterprise sales. Every enterprise prospect asks if we have an app. When we say mobile web, they look disappointed." Her view is that native app = credibility signal, and that's hard to quantify but real.

The Eng Lead, Priya, is neutral technically but has staffing concerns — pulling 2 engineers for 4-5 months will delay our Q3 roadmap items significantly.

The Sales team, specifically our Head of Sales Kieran, believes it affects close rates: "I've lost two enterprise deals in the last quarter to competitors who have apps. The procurement checklist literally has a checkbox for 'native mobile app.'"

We haven't decided. I want to make sure we've thought this through properly before we go to leadership. Can you help me structure this decision?

---

**What I'm worried we're missing:**

Honestly, I haven't thought deeply about whether there are options between "build a full native app" and "stay on mobile web." I feel like there might be a middle ground but I can't articulate it. And I'm not sure we've been honest with ourselves about what happens after we ship a native app — like, we can't just build it and walk away.

The leadership meeting is in 2 weeks. I need a structured brief that gets us to a decision.
