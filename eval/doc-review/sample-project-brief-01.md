# Project Brief: Borrower Re-engagement

**Owner:** [TBD]
**Date:** March 2026
**Status:** Early exploration

---

## The Opportunity

We're losing borrowers after their first loan is repaid. A lot of them never come back for a second loan, even when they're eligible. The hypothesis is that we're not doing enough to stay top-of-mind between loans, and by the time they need another one, they've already gone to a competitor.

There's also a secondary angle — some users drop off mid-application. They start an application, get to the income verification step, and abandon it. We don't know why, and we're not doing anything to bring them back.

Both of these feel related to the same root problem: we're not building a relationship with users between transactions. They think of us as a loan product, not a financial partner.

---

## What We're Thinking

The product team has been discussing a few different directions:

A re-engagement email series for post-repayment borrowers — something that keeps us present during the months between loans. Educational content, financial tips, a gentle reminder that they're eligible to apply again.

A push notification campaign targeting users who abandoned an application within the last 30 days. Personalized nudge with a deep link back to where they left off.

Maybe a loyalty angle — users who repay on time get some kind of benefit (lower rate, higher limit) on their next loan. This would need coordination with the credit and product teams.

Some of this might just be a marketing problem, not a product problem. We haven't aligned on that yet.

---

## Why Now

Leadership has flagged borrower retention as a Q2 priority. The data team has a new cohort analysis showing that second-loan conversion rates have dropped 12% year-over-year. We have pressure to show a plan before the Q2 planning lock.

---

## Open Questions

There's a lot we don't know. The data team is still pulling the full retention analysis. We don't have a clear owner for this — marketing, product, and credit all have a stake. And we're not sure which of the three directions above is the right starting point, or whether we should try to do all three at once.
